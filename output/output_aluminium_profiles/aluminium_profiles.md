## 1. Category Overview
Aluminium Profiles are engineered components created through the process of aluminium extrusion, where heated aluminium alloy is forced through a shaped die. The result is a semi-finished product with a specific cross-sectional profile, optimized for an exceptional balance of strength, light weight, and corrosion resistance. These profiles form the backbone of countless applications across Indian industries.

They are fundamental in construction for creating architectural partitions, sliding doors, and window systems. In the renewable energy sector, they provide the structural framework for [[Solar Panels]]. For modern interiors, they are integral to furniture, kitchen cabinetry, and modular systems, offering both functional support and aesthetic finishes. In manufacturing and transport, their strength-to-weight ratio is prized for building machine frames, commercial vehicle bodies, and precision components.

This category encompasses a vast range of products, from standard T-slot and L-angle shapes to complex, multi-part systems for specific end-uses like [[Aluminium Glass Railing]], cabinet shutters, and handle-less drawers. The Indian B2B market is highly diverse, serving massive infrastructure projects and small-scale fabricators alike, with procurement happening nationwide from manufacturing hubs like Gujarat and Karnataka.

## 2. Product Types & Variants
### 2.1 Main Product Types
*   **Structural Profiles:** Designed for load-bearing applications.
    *   **T-Slot / Strut Profiles:** These profiles feature one or more T-shaped slots (e.g., 8mm) and are the basis for constructing modular machine frames, workstations, safety guarding, and support structures. The PG30 series is a common example.
    *   **Solar Panel Mounting Profiles:** Specialized sections engineered for assembling photovoltaic module frames and mounting structures, designed for long-term outdoor exposure.

*   **Architectural Profiles:** Used in building construction and interior design for both functional and aesthetic purposes.
    *   **Partition & Door Profiles:** Comprehensive systems for creating office partitions, including single and double-sided panel systems. The category includes everything from the main frame to glazing beads and door sections.
    *   **Sliding Door & Window Profiles:** Multi-track frame and shutter systems (e.g., 2-track, 3-track) used to fabricate sliding assemblies for balconies, windows, and internal doors. This includes related components like [[Aluminium Door Profile]] sections and interlocks.
    *   **Facade & Railing Profiles:** Sections for curtain walls, building facades, and both framed and frameless [[Glass]] railing systems.

*   **Kitchen, Furniture & Cabinetry Profiles:** A system of specialized profiles for modern cabinetry and furniture.
    *   **Frame Profiles:** For constructing cabinet shutters, doors, and drawer fronts, often with integrated slots for hinges or handles.
    *   **Handle Profiles:** Profiles that function as integrated handles for drawers and cabinets, offering a sleek, hardware-free look.
    *   **G-Profile & Gola Profile:** Handle-less C-shaped or J-shaped profiles used in modular kitchen cabinetry to create a minimalist aesthetic.
    *   **Edge Profiles / Edge Banding:** U-channel profiles used to protect and finish the raw edges of furniture boards like [[MDF]] or [[Plywood]].
    *   **Center Profiles:** Vertically installed between two cabinet shutters, designed to hold glass or panels from both sides.

*   **Transport Profiles:** Specialized extruded sections for applications in the transport sector, including building commercial vehicle bodies, flooring, and structural components.

*   **Accessories & Companion Products:**
    *   **End Caps, Corner Brackets, Connectors:** Used to join profiles and provide a finished appearance to assemblies.
    *   **Gaskets:** Rubber or PVC seals, often pre-installed in furniture and partition profiles to secure glass or panels.
    *   **Hinges:** Specific hinges (e.g., 35mm cup-size cranked hinges) are often required for compatibility with certain frame profiles.

### 2.2 Available Configurations
*   **Standard Lengths:** Profiles are typically supplied in standard lengths or "strips," most commonly 3 meters (approx. 10 feet), 12 feet, 15 feet, and 20 feet.
*   **Finishes & Colours:**
    *   **Standard:** Mill Finish (raw aluminium), Black, Grey, Aluminium Anodized (natural matte).
    *   **Premium / Special Order:** Rose Gold, Champagne Gold, Stainless Steel (SS) Finish, Bronze.
    *   **Protective Coatings:** Powder Coating, Electrophoresis, and high-durability PVDF coatings for architectural use.
*   **System Design:** Profiles are often part of a series (e.g., 15MM Series, 18MM Series) where frames, shutters, and interlocks are designed to be fully compatible.

## 5. Pricing & Commercial Information
### 5.1 Price Overview
Pricing for aluminium profiles is highly variable and primarily determined by weight, finish, and order volume. The following are indicative market prices:

*   **Standard Profiles (per foot):**
    *   Mirror Profiles: ~₹45 per foot
    *   Glass Railing Profiles: ~₹150 (Top) to ~₹350 (Bottom) per foot
*   **Imported Decorative Profiles (per piece):** ₹680 – ₹800 per piece (length unspecified, typically 3m).
*   **Related Equipment:**
    *   Double Banding Machine: ~₹7,52,000
    *   Profile Cutting Machine: ~₹11,200

### 5.2 Pricing Factors
*   **Profile Weight & Complexity:** The primary driver of price is the linear mass density (kg/m). Heavier and more complex profiles are more expensive.
*   **Alloy & Temper:** The specific aluminium alloy and the heat treatment (temper) affect cost.
*   **Finish and Colour:** Premium finishes like Rose Gold, SS finish, and durable PVDF coatings command a higher price than standard mill finish or anodizing.
*   **Order Volume:** Significant price breaks are available for bulk quantities (tonnes) compared to retail or dealer quantities (pieces/bundles).
*   **Brand & Origin:** Profiles from Tier 1 manufacturers or imported from Europe often have a price premium over local or unbranded materials.
*   **GST:** Quoted prices may be GST inclusive or exclusive (typically 18%), which must be clarified during procurement.

## 6. Quality, Standards & Compliance
### 6.1 Applicable Standards
*   **Indian Standards:**
    *   **IS 1285:2002:** The key Indian standard for wrought aluminium and aluminium alloy extruded bars, rods, and sections.
    *   **IS 6051:1970:** Code for designation of aluminium and its alloys.
*   **Common Alloy Grades & Temper:**
    *   **6063 / HE-9:** The most common alloy, prized for its excellent surface finish characteristics, making it ideal for architectural applications and anodizing.
    *   **6061 / HE-20:** A stronger, more structural alloy often used for load-bearing applications like machine frames and transport sections.
    *   **Temper (T3-T8):** This designation specifies the heat treatment process, which determines the final mechanical properties like hardness and strength. T5 and T6 are very common.
*   **International / Foreign Standards:**
    *   **GB/T5237-2004:** Chinese national standard for wrought aluminium alloy extruded profiles for architecture.

### 6.2 Certifications & Approvals
Reputable manufacturers often hold certifications to demonstrate quality and process control, including:
*   **ISO 9001:** Quality Management Systems.
*   **ISO 14001:** Environmental Management Systems.
*   **CQM:** China Quality Mark Certification (for imported goods).
*   **ABS (American Bureau of Shipping):** For profiles intended for marine applications.

### 6.3 Quality Indicators for Buyers
*   **Alloy & Temper Verification:** Ensure the supplied material matches the specified alloy (e.g., 6063) and temper (e.g., T6) for the application's needs.
*   **Weight Consistency:** Check the weight per meter (kg/m). Consistent weight is a proxy for dimensional accuracy and material integrity.
*   **Finish Quality:** For architectural or decorative use, inspect the surface for uniformity, scratches, and finish defects. The quality of anodizing or powder coating is critical.
*   **Dimensional Tolerance:** Verify that profile dimensions are within the specified tolerances, especially for interlocking parts and system assemblies.
*   **Hardware Compatibility:** For system-based profiles (kitchen, windows), confirm compatibility with specified hinges, brackets, and connectors.

## 8. Supply Chain & Procurement
### 8.1 Ordering & MOQ
*   **Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ):** Varies significantly by supplier and product type. It can range from 20-30 pieces for standard profiles from a dealer, to 100+ pieces to qualify for wholesale pricing, up to bulk orders of 2-3 tonnes directly from a manufacturer for custom runs.
*   **Ordering Process:** Typically involves sharing a design or selecting from a digital catalog, followed by a formal quotation. For system profiles (e.g., kitchen or window systems), it's crucial to order all compatible components simultaneously.
*   **Supply Units:** Profiles are sold by length in units like "pieces" or "strips" (e.g., 3m, 12ft lengths). Bulk orders are often quoted and transacted by weight (per kg or per tonne).

### 8.2 Logistics & Handling
*   **Packaging:** Profiles are typically bundled and wrapped in plastic film to prevent scratches and surface damage during transit. Buyers should specify packaging requirements, especially for finished or decorative profiles.
*   **On-site Processing:** Most profiles are delivered in standard lengths and require cutting on-site. Furniture and frame applications often require precise 45° miter cuts for corner joints.

## 9. Market Intelligence
*   **Buyer & Seller Archetypes:**
    *   **Buyers:** Range from large EPCs and real estate developers procuring tonnes for major projects, to mid-sized system integrators (kitchens, windows), to local fabricators, wholesalers, and retailers buying in smaller bundles.
    *   **Sellers:** The market is tiered, with large mills (Hindalco, Jindal) selling to distributors and high-volume buyers, while specialized manufacturers (e.g., PAG, ALCOI) and regional players serve specific industries and geographies.
*   **Demand Signals:** There is strong B2B demand for complete systems (e.g., window + hardware, kitchen profiles + hinges), not just loose components. Aesthetic finishes like Stainless Steel (SS) look, Rose Gold, and matte black are highly requested for architectural and interior applications. For structural use, load-bearing capacity and alloy certification are key.
*   **Price Trends:** Pricing is closely tied to the LME (London Metal Exchange) aluminium price, but local factors like brand reputation, finish quality, and stock availability create significant variance. Bulk orders (tonnes) receive the best per-kg rates, while per-piece or per-foot pricing from dealers includes a significant markup. GST at 18% is a standard addition.
*   **Top 5 Requested Specifications:**
    1.  **Profile Type & Series:** (e.g., 3-track sliding window profile, 30x30 T-slot, Gola profile)
    2.  **Finish:** (e.g., Anodized, Powder Coated, SS Finish)
    3.  **Dimensions:** (e.g., Width, Height, board/glass thickness compatibility)
    4.  **Weight/Thickness:** (e.g., kg/meter, gauge)
    5.  **Brand/Origin:** (e.g., Jindal, Hindalco, Imported)

## Glossary
*   **Anodizing:** An electrochemical process that converts the metal surface into a durable, corrosion-resistant, decorative, anodic oxide finish.
*   **Extrusion:** A process used to create objects of a fixed cross-sectional profile by pushing material through a die of the desired shape.
*   **G-Profile / Gola Profile:** A handle-less aluminium profile, typically C-shaped or J-shaped, used in modern cabinetry to create a concealed grip.
*   **GB/T:** Recommended national standards issued by the Standardization Administration of China (SAC).
*   **IS (Indian Standard):** Standards formulated by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), the National Standards Body of India.
*   **Linear Mass Density:** The mass of a material per unit of length, typically expressed in kilograms per meter (kg/m). It is a key factor in determining a profile's weight, strength, and cost.
*   **Moment of Inertia (Ix, Iy):** A geometric property of a cross-section that measures its resistance to bending about a specific axis. Higher values indicate greater stiffness. Unit: cm⁴ or mm⁴.
*   **Section Modulus (Wx, Wy):** Also known as Moment of Resistance, it is a direct measure of a cross-section's strength against bending stress. Unit: cm³ or mm³.
*   **PVDF (Polyvinylidene Fluoride):** A high-performance fluoropolymer coating known for its excellent durability, colour retention, and resistance to weathering, used on architectural profiles.
*   **Temper:** A designation (e.g., T5, T6) that indicates the heat treatment and hardening process an aluminium alloy has undergone to achieve its final mechanical properties.
*   **T-Slot Profile:** A structural profile featuring a T-shaped channel designed for modular assembly with specialized nuts, bolts, and connectors.
