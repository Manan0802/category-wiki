## ✅ Self-Resolved Doubts

### DOUBT-701: 3. Technical Specifications → Moment of Inertia Unit *(Resolved)*

- **Originally raised at:** Step 7
- **Question:** The source document for SYSTEMS PG30 profiles lists the unit for Moment of Inertia (Ix, Iy) as cm³, but also includes a note stating this is a "unit likely a typo, standard unit is cm⁴". Is it correct to assume the standard unit (cm⁴) and correct it in the wiki, while noting the discrepancy?
- **Resolved at:** Step 10 (ENRICH)
- **Resolution:** The doubt regarding the unit for Moment of Inertia (Ix, Iy) is resolved. The source document listed it as 'cm³' but noted it was a likely typo. Standard engineering principles confirm that the unit for the second moment of area (Moment of Inertia) is length to the fourth power (cm⁴ or mm⁴). The unit for Section Modulus (Wx, Wy) is length to the third power (cm³), which was also listed in the source table. The original correction to cm⁴ in the wiki draft was accurate. The note in the final wiki has been updated to reflect this technical confirmation. [Reference: Standard Mechanical Engineering Principles]
