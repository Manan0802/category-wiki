## 1. Category Overview
### 1.1 Definition & Scope
Aluminium Profiles are engineered components created through the process of aluminium extrusion, where heated aluminium alloy is forced through a shaped die. The result is a semi-finished product with a specific cross-sectional profile, optimized for a balance of strength, light weight, and corrosion resistance. These profiles are used extensively in a wide range of applications, from structural frameworks and building facades to precision components in machinery. This category includes standard shapes, custom designs, and profiles intended for specific applications like railings, window frames, and modular structures.

### 1.2 Category Classification
Aluminium profiles can be broadly classified based on their application:
*   **Structural Profiles:** Designed for load-bearing applications, such as machine frames, support structures, and solar panel mounting. (e.g., T-slot profiles).
*   **Architectural Profiles:** Used in building construction and interior design for functional and aesthetic purposes. This includes door and window frames, curtain walls, facades, and railing systems.
*   **Decorative Profiles:** Primarily used for aesthetic enhancement, such as edging trims, skirting, and other finishing elements.
*   **Related Categories:** Aluminium Skirting Profiles, Aluminium Glass Railing, Structural Profiles, Aluminum Edging.

### 1.3 Market Context
The Indian B2B market for aluminium profiles is diverse, serving multiple industries including construction, manufacturing, automotive, and renewable energy. Buyers are typically contractors, builders, project managers, and manufacturers who procure these materials for commercial projects, turnkey contracts, or for integration into their own products. Procurement can range from small, one-time purchases of accessories to large, recurring bulk orders for ongoing construction sites or production lines.

## 2. Product Types & Variants
### 2.1 Main Product Types
*   **T-Slot Profiles:** Structural profiles featuring a "T" shaped slot, commonly used for building modular frames, workstations, and automation systems. They allow for easy assembly and connection of various components.
*   **Glass Railing Profiles:** Architectural profiles specifically designed for creating frameless glass railing systems. They are supplied as a set:
    *   **Top Profile:** A lighter channel that caps the top edge of the glass panel.
    *   **Bottom Profile:** A heavier, load-bearing channel that secures the base of the glass panel. A common design is locally referred to as "Machhali wala".
*   **Skirting Profiles:** Used at the junction of walls and floors.
*   **Decorative Profiles & Edging Trims:** Used for finishing and aesthetic purposes on furniture, walls, and other surfaces.
*   **Accessories:**
    *   **End Caps:** Used to provide a finished look to the open ends of profiles, particularly for skirting.

### 2.2 Available Sizes & Configurations
*   **T-Slot Profiles:** Available in square dimensions such as 60x60 mm and 40x40 mm.
*   **Railing Profiles:** Supplied in standard lengths, such as 15 feet per section.
