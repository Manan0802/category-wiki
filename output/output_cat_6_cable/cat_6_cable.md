## 1. Category Overview
Category 6 (Cat 6) cable is a standardized twisted-pair cable used for [[Ethernet]] and other physical network layers. Defined by the TIA/EIA-568 standard, it is fully backward compatible with previous standards like [[Cat 5e Cable|Cat 5e]], [[Cat 5 Cable]], and Cat 3. Cat 6 cables are engineered for high-speed data transmission, supporting digital and analog voice, video (RGB), and data signals within Local Area Networks (LANs). They offer superior performance compared to predecessors, featuring stricter specifications for reduced crosstalk and system noise.

These cables are fundamental for modern IT infrastructure, supporting data transfer speeds up to 1 Gbps over a standard 100-meter channel and up to 10 Gbps over shorter distances (up to 55 meters). They are optimized for protocols such as 1000BASE-T (Gigabit Ethernet), 100BASE-TX (Fast Ethernet), and high-speed ATM, making them a staple in [[structured cabling]] systems.

In the Indian B2B market, Cat 6 cables are a core component for IT infrastructure projects of all scales. Procurement is driven by new construction, office fit-outs, data center builds, and network upgrades. Key buyers include IT system integrators, electrical contractors, and corporate IT departments, serving end-users from small offices to large industrial facilities.

## 2. Product Types & Variants
The primary differentiation in Cat 6 cables is based on shielding, jacket material, and application.

*   **U/UTP (Unshielded/Unshielded Twisted Pair):** The most common and cost-effective variant, often called UTP. It consists of four unshielded twisted pairs, typically separated by a central plastic spline to minimize crosstalk. It is ideal for standard office environments where electromagnetic interference (EMI) is not a major concern. The outer jacket is usually made of [[PVC]].

*   **SFTP (Shielded Foiled Twisted Pair):** Also designated S/FTP, these cables offer superior protection against EMI and crosstalk. Each twisted pair is individually wrapped in a foil shield, and an overall braided shield (typically tinned [[copper]]) encases all four pairs. This robust construction makes SFTP cables suitable for high-interference settings like data centers, industrial plants, or installations near heavy power cables. They are available with [[FR-PVC]] or [[LSZH]] outer jackets.

*   **Outdoor Armored Cable:** This specialized variant is designed for maximum physical and environmental protection. It features a double-jacket construction (e.g., an inner LSZH sheath and an outer PE sheath) with an intermediate layer of metal armor, such as Electrically Continuous Corrugated Steel (ECCS) tape. This design provides resistance to moisture, rodents, and physical damage, making it perfect for direct burial or harsh outdoor installations.

*   **Patch Cords:** These are pre-terminated cables of various standard lengths (e.g., 9 inches, 2 meters) with [[RJ45 Connector|RJ45 connectors]] factory-installed. Unlike bulk cables with solid conductors, patch cords use stranded conductors for enhanced flexibility. They are used for connecting devices to wall outlets or patching connections in server racks and may feature durable plug designs for high-use scenarios.

### 3.3 Specification Interdependencies
*   **Solid vs. Stranded Conductor:** Solid core cable (typically 23 AWG) is used for permanent "backbone" installations. It offers lower attenuation and is ideal for structured cabling runs in walls and ceilings. Stranded core cable (typically 24 AWG) is more flexible and is used for shorter patch cords that require frequent handling.
*   **Speed vs. Distance:** While Cat 6 supports 10 Gbps, this speed is only reliably achieved over shorter distances (up to 55 meters). For the maximum standard channel length of 100 meters, the reliable speed is 1 Gbps. For guaranteed 10 Gbps over 100m, a [[Cat 6A Cable]] is required.
*   **Shielding vs. Environment:** The choice between U/UTP and SFTP is determined by the ambient EMI. Using U/UTP in a high-EMI factory can degrade performance, whereas using expensive shielded cables in a standard office yields no practical advantage.
*   **Jacket Material vs. Safety:** LSZH jackets are specified for enclosed public spaces, data centers, and transport hubs where fire safety and low smoke emission are critical. Standard PVC is suitable for general-purpose installations.

## 5. Pricing & Commercial Information
Pricing for Cat 6 cable in the Indian B2B market is influenced by brand reputation, conductor material, shielding, and order volume.

*   **Bulk Pricing (305m drum/box):**
    *   **Economy/Entry-Level:** ₹2,700 – ₹5,500. This range typically includes lesser-known brands or standard UTP cables from major players like D-Link.
    *   **Mid-Range:** ₹6,000 – ₹8,000. This is the common price band for reputable brands like Polycab, Havells, and standard Commscope UTP cables.
    *   **Premium/Specialty:** ₹8,000 – ₹10,700+. This includes high-performance cables from brands like Commscope (SYSTIMAX), Belden, and specialty types like SFTP, Armored, or LSZH variants.
*   **Per-Meter Pricing:** For smaller, custom-cut lengths, rates are typically around ₹65 per meter, though this is less common in B2B procurement.
*   **GST:** An 18% GST is typically applicable on these products. Buyers must confirm if quoted prices are inclusive or exclusive of GST.

**Key Pricing Factors:**
*   **Brand Reputation:** Tier 1 brands consistently command a premium over Tier 2 and Tier 3 brands.
*   **Conductor Material:** Cables with 100% solid bare copper conductors are more expensive and perform significantly better than non-compliant Copper Clad Aluminium (CCA) alternatives. Authenticity of the copper is a primary price driver.
*   **Shielding & Armor:** SFTP and Armored cables are considerably more expensive than standard UTP cables due to additional materials and manufacturing complexity.
*   **Jacket Material:** LSZH and other fire-rated jackets are priced higher than standard PVC.
*   **Certifications:** Third-party verification from labs like UL or ETL adds to the cost but guarantees performance and safety compliance.

## 6. Quality, Standards & Compliance
Cat 6 cable performance and safety are governed by a range of international and material-specific standards.

*   **Performance Standards:**
    *   **ANSI/TIA-568-C.2:** The primary North American standard defining performance and testing requirements for Cat 6 cables.
    *   **ISO/IEC 11801:** The international standard for generic cabling in commercial premises.
    *   **EN 50173-1:** The corresponding European standard for generic cabling systems.
*   **Safety & Material Standards:**
    *   **UL 94V-0:** A flamability standard for plastics used in connectors and jackets, indicating self-extinguishing properties.
    *   **IEC 60332-1:** A test for vertical flame propagation on a single insulated wire or cable.
    *   **IEC 60754-1 & -2:** Tests for halogen acid gas content and conductivity, critical for LSZH compliance.
    *   **IEC 61034-1 & -2:** Measurement of smoke density during combustion, also for LSZH cables.
    *   **IEC 62321:** Test methods for regulated substances, related to RoHS compliance.

**Key Quality Indicators for Buyers:**
*   **Authenticity:** Purchase from authorized dealers to avoid counterfeit products, especially for leading brands.
*   **Cable Jacket Printing:** Legitimate cables feature clear, legible printing including brand, cable type (Cat 6), standards compliance (e.g., TIA/EIA-568-C.2), conductor gauge (AWG), and sequential meter markings.
*   **Conductor Material:** Always specify and verify 100% solid bare copper conductors. Avoid Copper Clad Aluminium (CCA), as it is non-compliant, less reliable, and has higher signal loss.
*   **Physical Construction:** A quality cable has a durable, uniform jacket, a central spline for pair separation, and a nylon rip cord for easy stripping.

## 8. Supply Chain & Procurement
**8.1 Ordering & MOQ**
The standard Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) in the B2B channel is a single bulk box or drum, containing 305 meters (1000 feet) of cable. Installers and system integrators purchase in these quantities for on-site termination. Patch cords are available individually or in packs.

**8.2 Logistics & Handling**
Bulk cable is typically packaged in a cardboard "pull box" for easy, tangle-free dispensing. A standard box weighs approximately 13-14 kg. Heavier armored cables are supplied on plastic or wooden reels. The packaging is designed for standard transport but should be protected from moisture.

**8.3 Storage**
Cables should be stored indoors in a cool, dry environment. Long-term exposure to direct sunlight (UV radiation), moisture, or extreme temperatures can degrade the jacket material and negatively impact electrical performance.

## 9. Market Intelligence
**9.1 Buyer & Seller Archetypes**
*   **Sellers:** The supply side consists of manufacturer-authorized national distributors, regional dealers, and specialized cable suppliers. Premium brands (Commscope, Belden) enforce strict channel partner programs, while other brands are more widely available through general electrical and IT hardware suppliers.
*   **Buyers:**
    *   **System Integrators & Installers:** The largest buyer segment. They purchase in bulk for projects and prioritize consistency, standards compliance, and ease of termination. Brand is often dictated by project specifications.
    *   **Corporate IT Departments:** Procure cables for in-house network maintenance and expansion. They prioritize brand reputation, reliability, and often have preferred brands.
    *   **Electrical Contractors:** Purchase for new construction and renovation projects, often bundling networking cables with other electrical supplies.
    *   **SMEs & SOHO:** Smaller businesses that buy a few boxes for office setups, often prioritizing price and availability from local suppliers.

**9.2 Demand Signals & Price Trends**
*   **Demand Signals:** The most frequently requested specifications are "23 AWG solid copper," "U/UTP," and "305-meter drum." There is growing demand for LSZH jackets in metro areas and for projects with strict fire safety codes. Authenticity ("original D-Link," "original Commscope") is a critical qualifier.
*   **Price Trends:** Pricing is stable but sensitive to fluctuations in global copper prices. A clear price segmentation exists: premium international brands command the highest price, followed by major Indian manufacturers, with smaller players and imported cables at the lower end. The price difference between genuine copper and non-compliant CCA cable is significant, creating a market for low-cost, low-performance alternatives.

**9.3 Top 5 Requested Specifications**
1.  **Conductor:** 23 AWG Solid Bare Copper
2.  **Shielding:** U/UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair)
3.  **Packaging:** 305 Meter Pull Box / Drum
4.  **Brand Tier:** Preference for Tier 1 (Commscope) or Tier 2 (Polycab, D-Link) depending on budget.
5.  **Jacket Type:** Standard PVC, with LSZH as a common secondary requirement.

**9.4 Competitive Landscape**
The primary competitor for Cat 6 cable is its predecessor, **[[Cat 5e Cable]]**, which is still sufficient for some 1 Gbps applications at a lower cost. For new installations requiring higher performance or future-proofing, the alternative is **[[Cat 6A Cable]]**, which supports 10 Gbps over the full 100-meter channel. The most significant threat within the category comes from counterfeit and non-compliant CCA cables, which undermine market pricing and performance expectations.

**9.5 Regional Patterns & Procurement Insights**
*   **Regional Patterns:** Manufacturing is concentrated in industrial zones in states like Gujarat and Maharashtra. Major consumption hubs are Tier-1 and Tier-2 cities with high concentrations of corporate offices, IT parks, and data centers, such as Bengaluru, Mumbai, Delhi-NCR, Pune, Hyderabad, and Chennai.
*   **Procurement Insights:** Demand is not seasonal but project-based, tying directly to construction, corporate relocation, and IT refresh cycles. Bulk discounts are standard practice for large-volume purchases. Buyers often establish relationships with specific distributors to ensure consistent supply and pricing.

## 10. Buyer Intelligence
**10.1 Common Buyer Questions & Decision Criteria**
*   **Authenticity:** *"Is this an original Commscope/D-Link product?"* This is the top concern, as counterfeit products are prevalent.
*   **Conductor Material:** *"Is it 100% pure copper or CCA?"* Experienced buyers know to verify the conductor material as the most critical quality indicator.

*   **Compliance:** *"Does it meet TIA standards?"* This confirms the cable will perform as expected within a structured cabling system.

*   LAN Cable
*   Ethernet Cable
*   Networking Cable

*   *\[Brand Name]* Cable (e.g., D-Link Cable, Commscope Cable)

**10.3 Common Concerns**
The single greatest procurement challenge is verifying product authenticity and avoiding substandard cables. The market is flooded with low-quality Copper Clad Aluminium (CCA) cables that are often falsely marketed as Cat 6. These cables fail to meet performance standards, are not approved by TIA/EIA, and can lead to unreliable networks, data loss, and troubleshooting nightmares. Buyers are therefore highly cautious and often rely on trusted suppliers or demand proof of authenticity.

## Glossary
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|

| **AWG** | American Wire Gauge. A standard for measuring wire diameter. A lower AWG number indicates a thicker wire. |
| **Cat 6** | Category 6. A performance standard for twisted pair network cabling. |
| **CCA** | Copper Clad Aluminium. A conductor with an aluminium core coated in copper. It is cheaper, has higher resistance, and is non-compliant with TIA standards for Cat 6. |
| **EMI** | Electromagnetic Interference. Electrical noise from external sources that can disrupt signals in a cable. |
| **FR-PVC** | Flame-Retardant Polyvinyl Chloride. A cable jacket that resists ignition and slows the spread of fire. |
| **Gbps** | Gigabits per second. A measure of data transfer speed. |
| **HDPE** | High Density Polyethylene. A durable plastic commonly used for wire insulation. |
| **IEC** | International Electrotechnical Commission. An international standards organization. |
| **LSZH** | Low Smoke Zero Halogen. A cable jacket material that emits limited smoke and no toxic halogen compounds when exposed to fire. |
| **MHz** | Megahertz. A measure of frequency, indicating the bandwidth capacity of the cable. Cat 6 is rated for 250 MHz. |
| **NEXT** | Near-End Crosstalk. Signal interference between pairs, measured at the same end as the signal source. |
| **PoE** | Power over Ethernet. A technology that allows network cables to carry electrical power. |
| **PS-NEXT** | Power Sum Near-End Crosstalk. The combined NEXT from all interfering pairs onto a single victim pair. |
| **PVC**| Polyvinyl Chloride. A common, durable plastic used for the outer jacket of many cables. |
| **RJ45** | Registered Jack 45. The standard 8-pin modular connector used to terminate Ethernet cables. |
| **RoHS** | Restriction of Hazardous Substances. A directive restricting the use of specific hazardous materials. |

| **SYSTIMAX** | A prominent product line of structured cabling solutions from Commscope. |
| **TIA/EIA-568** | A set of telecommunications standards from the TIA that define structured cabling standards. |
