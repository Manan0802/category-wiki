## 1. Category Overview
**1.1 Definition & Scope**
Category 6 (Cat 6) cable is a standardized twisted pair cable for Ethernet and other network physical layers. It is defined by the TIA/EIA-568 standard and is backward compatible with previous standards like Cat 5, Cat 5e, and Cat 3. Cat 6 cables are designed to deliver significantly higher performance, with reduced crosstalk and system noise. They support data transfer speeds up to 10 Gigabits per second (Gbps) over limited distances, making them suitable for most modern commercial and residential networking applications.

**1.2 Category Classification**
Cat 6 cable is a sub-category within the broader classifications of Networking Cables, LAN Cables, and Ethernet Cables. It represents a specific performance standard within this family.

**1.3 Market Context**
In the Indian B2B market, Cat 6 cables are a fundamental component for IT infrastructure projects. They are procured by a wide range of professionals, including IT technicians, system integrators, electricians, and construction contractors. End-users span from small offices and residential complexes to large corporate buildings, data centers, and industrial facilities. Procurement is frequent, driven by new construction, office fit-outs, and network upgrades.

## 2. Product Types & Variants
**2.1 Main Product Types**
The primary differentiation in Cat 6 cables is based on shielding:

*   **UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair):** This is the most common and cost-effective type, suitable for general office and home environments where electromagnetic interference (EMI) is not a significant concern. The pairs are twisted to help mitigate crosstalk.
*   **SFTP (Shielded Foiled Twisted Pair):** These cables include both an overall braided shield and individual foil shielding around each twisted pair. They offer superior protection against EMI and crosstalk, making them ideal for high-interference environments such as industrial settings, data centers, or installations near heavy power cables.

**2.2 Available Sizes & Configurations**
*   **Bulk Cable:** The standard B2B packaging is a 305-meter (approximately 1,000 feet) length supplied in a pull-out box or on a plastic drum. This allows installers to cut custom lengths for structured cabling runs.
*   **Patch Cords:** Pre-terminated cables of various standard lengths (e.g., 3 ft, 5 ft, 10 ft, 25 ft, 50 ft) with RJ45 connectors already installed. These are used for connecting devices to wall outlets or for patching connections in a server rack.

## 6. Quality, Standards & Compliance
**6.1 Applicable Standards**
Cat 6 cable performance is primarily governed by international standards to ensure interoperability and performance.
*   **TIA/EIA-568-B.2-1:** The key standard from the Telecommunications Industry Association that defines the performance and testing requirements for Cat 6 cables.
*   **ISO/IEC 11801:** An international standard for generic cabling for customer premises, which also specifies performance criteria for Cat 6.

**6.2 Certifications & Approvals**
*   **UL (Underwriters Laboratories) / ETL (Intertek):** Certifications that verify the cable meets safety and performance standards. A UL mark is a strong indicator of quality.
*   **RoHS (Restriction of Hazardous Substances):** Compliance indicates the cable is free from specific hazardous materials.

**6.3 Quality Indicators for Buyers**
*   **Authenticity:** For brands like D-Link, buyers are often concerned about receiving "original" products. Requesting proof of authenticity or buying from authorized dealers is common.
*   **Print on Cable Jacket:** Genuine cables will have clear, legible printing that includes the brand name, cable type (Cat 6), standard compliance, and AWG.
*   **Conductor Material:** Insist on solid bare copper conductors for best performance. Avoid Copper Clad Aluminium (CCA) which is less reliable and not compliant with TIA standards for Cat 6.
*   **Jacket Quality:** The outer jacket should be durable and evenly applied. FR (Flame Retardant) properties are important for safety.
