### DOUBT-2801: 1. Category Overview → 1.1 Definition & Scope / 1.2 Category Classification *(Resolved)*

- **Question:** The new source (pdf 11.json) details an "Everest Hi-Tech Corrugated Sheet" conforming to BIS 14871:2000, which is the standard for "Coated cement corrugated sheets". This product is made of Fibre Cement, not metal (PPGI/PPGL/Aluminum) like all previous sources. Should "Colour Coated Roofing Sheet" as a category officially include non-metallic bases like fibre cement, or should this product be classified under a separate category like "Fibre Cement Roofing Sheets"?
- **Resolved at:** Step 42 (ENRICH)
- **Resolution:** The category "Colour Coated Roofing Sheet" is best defined by its function and market perception rather than strictly by its base material. Buyers seeking roofing solutions often compare metal and non-metallic options like fibre cement based on project needs (e.g., aesthetics, insulation, weight, cost). Both product types are available "colour coated" and are often sold through similar channels. Including fibre cement sheets as a distinct non-metallic variant within this broader category provides a more comprehensive and useful comparison for a B2B buyer, reflecting real-world procurement considerations. The distinction between metallic (PPGI/PPGL) and non-metallic (Fibre Cement) bases will be clearly maintained throughout the article to prevent confusion. This is supported by the fact that a major brand like Everest, known for fibre cement, is often mentioned by buyers in the context of roofing sheets generally.
