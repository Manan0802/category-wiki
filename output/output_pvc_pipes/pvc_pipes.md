## 1. Category Overview
### 1.1 Definition & Scope
Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) Pipes are a versatile and widely used type of plastic pipe primarily for conveying water and other fluids. As a fundamental component in modern infrastructure, they are essential for plumbing, irrigation, and sanitation projects. The category primarily consists of pipes made from Unplasticized PVC (uPVC or PVC-U), a rigid and durable material ideal for pressure applications. Some variants are made from `[[PVC-Res樹脂]]` that is UV-stabilized to prevent degradation from sunlight, a crucial feature for agricultural and surface applications.

The product scope is broad, covering everything from drinking water supply and agricultural irrigation to specialized uses. Key sub-categories include **uPVC Pressure Pipes** for water mains, **uPVC Column Pipes** designed for high-tensile loads in `[[Submersible-Pump]]` installations, **SWR (Soil, Waste, and Rainwater) Pipes** for drainage, and **Casing Pipes** for lining borewells. Manufacturers consistently highlight features such as corrosion resistance, smooth internal walls for high flow rates, low installation costs, and a long service life often exceeding 50 years.

-   **Parent Category**: Plumbing & Sanitation > Pipes & Fittings
-   **Sibling/Related Categories**: Complementary products include `[[PVC-Pipe-Fittings]]`, `[[Solvent-Cement]]`, and `[[Rubber-Gasket]]`. ## 2. Product Types & Variants
### 2.1 Main Product Types
PVC pipes are categorized based on their application, material properties, and jointing method.

-   **Unplasticized PVC (uPVC/PVC-U) Pressure Pipes**: The most common type, used for potable water supply, irrigation, and industrial fluid transport. They are classified by pressure ratings (kg/cm² or Classes) and conform to **IS 4985**. They are joined using `[[Solvent-Cement]]` or gasketed joints.
-   **uPVC Column Pipes**: Specialized high-tensile pipes designed as the riser pipe for `[[Submersible-Pump]]` installations in borewells. Their key features are high load-bearing capacity, precision-machined square threads, and locking mechanisms to prevent detachment. They are typically available in "Standard" and "Heavy" grades to suit various pump depths and weights.
-   **SWR (Soil, Waste, and Rainwater) Pipes**: Designed for gravity-fed drainage systems in buildings, conforming to **IS 13592**. Not suitable for pressure applications.
    -   **Type A**: Used for ventilating stacks and rainwater harvesting.
    -   **Type B**: Have thicker walls for higher strength, used for discharging soil and waste.
-   **Schedule Pipes (Sch 40, 80, 120)**: Classified per **ASTM D1785**, these pipes have a standard outside diameter for a given size, but wall thickness increases with the schedule number, enabling higher pressure tolerance. Common in industrial applications and plumbing.
-   **Clear PVC Pipes**: A transparent variant, typically with Schedule 40 dimensions, used in applications requiring visual monitoring of fluid flow, such as sight glasses, lab work, and low-pressure process lines.
-   **CM & CS Casing Pipes**: Designed specifically for borewell construction, conforming to **IS 12818**. CM (Casing Medium) is for medium-depth wells, and CS (Casing Shallow) is for shallower wells.
-   **PVC Agriculture (Agri) Pipes**: Intended for irrigation and agricultural pump sets, typically ISI marked and specified by diameter and a "kg" pressure rating.
-   **PVC Conduit Pipes**: Used as a protective casing for `[[Electrical-Wire]]` systems in construction.

### 2.2 Classification by Joint Type
-   **Plain End / Selfit (Solvent Weld)**: Pipes with either straight ends (Plain) or one factory-formed socketed end (Selfit). Both are permanently joined using `[[Solvent-Cement]]`. Selfit pipes can reduce installation time and `[[Solvent-Cement]]` consumption by up to 50%.
-   **Bell End / Ringfit (Gasketed)**: Pipes with one flared "bell" end containing a groove for a `[[Rubber-Gasket]]`. This creates a push-fit, leak-proof seal that accommodates thermal expansion and contraction, common in SWR and underground water mains.

### 2.3 Available Sizes & Configurations
-   **Diameter**: The range is extensive, from small plumbing lines to large water mains.
    -   **Buyer Requests (common sizes)**: 25mm (~1"), 75mm (~3"), 100mm (4"), 125mm (5"), 200mm (8"), 250mm (10"), 315mm (12").
    -   **Manufacturer Offerings**: Standard Outside Diameters (OD) range from 20mm up to 400mm. Column pipes are available in bore sizes from 25mm (1") to 100mm (4").
-   **Length**:
    -   **Commonly Requested**: 10 feet (~3m), 20 feet (~6m).
    -   **Manufacturer Standards**: Standard lengths are **3m, 4m, 5.8m, and 6m**.
-   **Colors**: **Grey** is the most common colour for uPVC pressure pipes, followed by **White**. SWR pipes are typically grey. Other colors like **Blue** can be supplied upon request. Clear pipes are transparent.
-   **Quantity**: Procured by length ("meters"), units ("pieces"), or "bundles".

### 3.2 Specification Interdependencies
-   **Pressure, Wall Thickness, and Cost**: For a given diameter, a higher pressure rating (e.g., Class 3 / 6 kg/cm²) necessitates a thicker wall, which increases the material weight and a pipe's per-meter cost.
-   **Column Pipe Grade & Performance**: A "Heavy" grade column pipe has a thicker wall than a "Standard" grade of the same diameter. This results in a significantly higher Ultimate Breaking Load, Safe Pulling Load, and allows it to support a greater Pump Delivery Head, making it suitable for deeper borewells.
-   **SWR Type A vs. Type B**: For SWR drainage pipes, Type B has a thicker wall than Type A of the same diameter, providing the necessary strength for carrying soil and solid waste, while Type A is sufficient for rainwater and ventilation.
-   **Joint Type & Application**: Ringfit (gasketed) joints are preferred for buried non-pressure lines (like main drains) as they handle soil movement and thermal expansion. Selfit (solvent weld) joints form a rigid, monolithic structure ideal for above-ground plumbing and pressure lines.

## 6. Quality, Standards & Compliance
### 6.1 Applicable Standards
-   **Indian Standards (BIS)**:
    -   **IS 4985:2021**: The benchmark standard for uPVC pipes for potable water supplies.
    -   **IS 13592**: Specifies requirements for uPVC SWR drainage systems.
    -   **IS 12818:1992**: Specifies requirements for uPVC Casing Pipes for borewells.
    -   **IS 12231:1987**: Covers uPVC pipes for suction and delivery lines of agricultural pumps.
-   **International Standards**:
    -   **ASTM D1785**: U.S. standard for Schedule 40, 80, and 120 PVC pipes.
    -   **ISO 161/1, ISO 4422**: International standards for thermoplastics pipes.
    -   **GB/T 18742.2-2002**: A Chinese national standard.

### 6.2 Certifications & Approvals
-   **ISI Mark**: The most critical certification in the Indian market, signifying compliance with BIS standards. It is a mandatory requirement for government projects and a key quality indicator for buyers.
-   **ISO Certifications**: Reputable manufacturers often hold **ISO 9001** (Quality Management) and **ISO 14001** (Environmental Management) certifications.

### 6.3 Quality Indicators for Buyers
-   **Verify the ISI Mark**: The primary assurance of quality and adherence to Indian standards.
-   **Match Pipe to Application**: Clearly specify the use (pressure, SWR, column pipe) and required rating (Class, Schedule, Grade) to ensure fitness-for-purpose.
-   **For Column Pipes**: Inspect for CNC-machined **square threads** for better load capacity. Ask about the **locking mechanism** for couplers and verify the presence of **EPDM 'O' rings**.
-   **Review Technical Data Sheets**: For critical applications, request datasheets to verify properties like Vicat Softening Temperature, wall thickness, safe working stress, and chemical resistance.
-   **Confirm Joint Type**: Select between Selfit (solvent weld) and Ringfit (gasketed) based on installation environment and crew expertise.

-   **Order Process**: The typical B2B workflow begins with a phone inquiry, followed by sharing a rate list, catalogue, and product photos on WhatsApp. Formal procurement may involve GST verification and a formal RFX process.

### 8.2 Logistics & Handling
-   **Transport Cost**: Freight is a major cost factor. For inter-state transport, main freight costs may be included in bulk quotes. For smaller orders or remote locations, high logistics costs make local sourcing the only practical option.
-   **Distributor Network**: Major brands mitigate high logistics costs and ensure nationwide product availability through a robust network of regional distributors and dealers.

### 8.3 Storage
Proper storage is crucial to maintain pipe integrity and prevent damage:
-   **Support**: Store pipes on a level, flat surface. Use horizontal wooden battens spaced no more than 1 meter apart to prevent sagging. Do not store directly on uneven ground.
-   **Stacking**: Stack pipes in layers, with larger diameters at the bottom. To prevent deformation of the lower layers, the stack height should not exceed 1.5 meters.
-   **Protection**: Protect pipes from direct sunlight to prevent UV degradation. If stored outdoors, cover the stack with a light-colored, opaque tarp that allows for air circulation. Avoid dark tarps that absorb heat.
-   **Handling**: Avoid dropping pipes or dragging them on rough surfaces. Keep them away from sharp objects, hot surfaces, and heavy machinery.
-   **Inventory**: Practice "First-In, First-Out" (FIFO) to ensure older stock is used first.
