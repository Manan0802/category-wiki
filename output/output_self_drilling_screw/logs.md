## 1. Category Overview
### 1.1 Definition & Scope
A Self-Drilling Screw is a type of fastener that features a drill-shaped point. This unique design allows it to drill its own hole, cut or form mating threads, and fasten materials in a single, efficient operation, eliminating the need for pre-drilling. In the Indian market, they are widely known by the abbreviation "SGS Screw". These screws are primarily used for joining metal to metal or metal to other materials like wood.

### 1.2 Category Classification
- **Path**: Hardware & Tools > Fasteners > Screws > Self Drilling Screw

### 1.3 Market Context
Self-drilling screws are a high-demand B2B product in India, driven by the construction, manufacturing, and infrastructure sectors. Typical buyers include hardware shopkeepers purchasing for resale, as well as contractors and companies involved in Pre-Engineered Buildings (PEB), solar panel installation, and general fabrication. Procurement often involves large, wholesale quantities.

## 2. Product Types & Variants
### 2.1 Main Product Types
Self-drilling screws are available in various configurations, primarily distinguished by their head type and specialized features.

**By Head Type:**
- **Hex Head**: Offers excellent grip for tools, allowing for higher torque application. Common in roofing and structural applications.
- **Pan Head**: A low-profile dome-shaped head with a flat bearing surface, suitable for general-purpose metal fastening.
- **Countersunk (CSK) Head**: A conical head that sits flush with the material surface, used where a smooth finish is required.
- **Wafer Head**: A large, flat head providing a wide bearing surface, ideal for fixing thin materials without pull-through.
- **Truss Head**: A wide, low-profile dome head that offers a larger bearing surface than a pan head.

**By Specialization:**
- **Roofing Screws**: Typically hex-headed screws assembled with an EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer) washer to create a weatherproof seal.
- **Winged Self-Drilling Screws**: Feature small wings between the threads and the drill point. These wings ream a clearance hole in softer materials like wood or composites, then break off when they hit the metal substrate, allowing the screw to thread securely.
- **Screws for PUF Panels**: Designed for fastening sandwich panels, often with longer lengths and specific thread designs.
