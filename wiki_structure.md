## How to use this guide

Each section below defines what to cover and why it matters. Fill every mandatory section from your source documents. If a section has no data, write `> 📭 No data found` — never skip the heading. For conditional sections, include them only if your sources contain meaningful data. Do NOT invent or add new top-level sections out of your own judgment.

## 1. Category Overview
*(Mandatory)*

### 1.1 Definition & Scope
What this category is, in plain language. Cover what the product is made of, how it works, and what it does. State clearly what is **included** and what is **excluded** from this category. Use `[[wiki links]]` to related categories at the boundary of scope.

### 1.2 Category Classification
Where this category sits in the marketplace taxonomy.
- Parent and sub-categories
- Sibling categories at the same level
- Related/complementary categories

### 1.3 Market Context
The business environment for this category.
- Which industries heavily use these products
- Critical business applications (why buyers need this)
- Target buyer and seller profiles:
  - Company sizes (SME, Mid-market, Enterprise)
  - Industries (Manufacturing, Construction, Infrastructure, etc.)
- Frequency of purchase (one-time, recurring, project-based)
